Math 363 Learning Outcomes and Homework


Homework Policy

Homework assignments for each learning outcome, consisting of problems from the required textbook, will be posted below throughout the semester. Some questions are for turning in, and some are for extra practice. For a given assignment, you should submit all ‘turn-in’ problems, and feedback on your solutions will be provided by me or the TA. If you submit an incorrect or incomplete solution, you will have the opportunity to resubmit for full credit. All homework papers should be neatly written, stapled, and free from perforated note- book holes. Any assignment not in this condition will not be accepted. Satisfactorily completing 80% of homework assignments will earn you an M in LO29.

Among the problems you turn in, I will ask the TA to do a few things:

  • Grade a subset of 4-6 of the problems. If you are missing a crucial piece, or don’t seem to understand the concepts, the TA will make a comment and will not get credit for that problem.
  • Check to see that you did all the problems. Ungraded problems that you turned in are graded on effort only.
  • On the front of the homework, you will get “Satisfactory” (S) or “Not Satisfactory” (NS). If you get NS, there will be a comment about what you should do to get a satisfactory score (e.g., "finish all problems", "correct #2.13", etc.). In general, you will just need to redo problems you didn’t complete or missed to earn a satisfactory score. Please redo problems on a new sheet of paper, and turn in your old assignment with the corrected work.

Probability Learning Outcomes
  • LO1: I can describe sample spaces and/or events by listing all simple events they contain.
  • LO2: I can use properties of unions, intersections, and/or complements to find the probability of compound events.
    • Extra Practice: 2.95, 2.96
  • LO3: I can calculate probabilities of events using counting techniques.
    • Extra Practice: 2.39, 2.42, 2.51, 2.55, 2.60(a), 2,1.20, 2.121
  • LO4: I can employ the techniques of conditional probability to find the probability of events.
    • Extra Practice: 2.71, 2.75, 2.77, 2.78, 2.83, 2.95, 2.114, 2.125
  • LO5: I can determine if two events are independent and/or disjoint.
    • Extra Practice: 2.74
  • LO6: I can use probability rules and axioms to prove statements about probability or independence.
    • Extra Practice: 2.96, 2.91, 2.104
Distributions of Random Variables
  • LO7: I can express the probability distribution of a discrete random variable using a function or table.
    • Extra Practice: 2.140
  • LO8: I can apply the best probability distribution to model a given phenomenon.
  • LO9: I can calculate the probability of events involving discrete random variables.
  • LO10: I can calculate probabilities of events involving continuous random variables.
  • LO11: I can derive the moment generating function (MGF) of a random variable from the definition of MGF.
  • LO12: I can use the moment generating function to answer questions about a random variable.
  • LO13: I can find the expected value/variance associated with a given random variable using properties of expected value/variance.
  • LO14: I can convert between a random variable’s probability mass/density function and its cumulative distribution function.
Multivariate Distributions
  • LO15: I can use a joint distribution to calculate probabilities of events involving several random variables.
  • LO16: I can derive conditional and marginal distributions from the joint distribution of a random vector.
  • LO17: I can determine if two random variables are independent.
  • LO18: I can compute the covariance and correlation of jointly distributed random variables.
  • LO19: I can compute the expected value and variance for linear functions of dependent RVs.
  • LO20: I can compute conditional expectations and conditional variances of a random variable.
  • LO21: I can apply a variety of methods to obtain the probability distribution of a function of one random variable.
  • LO22: I can apply a variety of methods to obtain the probability distribution of a function of several random variables.
  • LO23: I can calculate probabilities involving order statistics.
Estimation and the Central Limit Theorem
  • LO24: I can apply the Central Limit Theorem to find the distribution of sample statistics.
  • LO25: I can use a sampling distribution to provide estimates for population parameters.
  • LO26: I can evaluate the effectiveness of an estimator using bias and mean square error.
  • LO27: I can calculate an estimator for a parameter of interest using the Method of Moments.
